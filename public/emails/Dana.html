DANA (+)
Staying on Target
TP: USD21 • Upside: 29% • Price: USD16.3 (20 Feb. 25)

DAN's 4Q24 print aligned with its prelim. results, with few surprises as it moves toward an expected Off-Highway sale in early 2Q25. The sale will dramatically transform DAN's balance sheet to net cash - as its cost savings equally transform profitability & FCF. We see robust 3-yr. shareholder returns capacity of ~$1.5B (~62% of current mkt. cap.), w/ net leverage rising to a mere ~0.8x. With the shares currently trading at just ~3.1x EV/EBITDA on a 2026E pro-forma basis, we continue to see significant upside potential; reiterate Outperform ($21 PT).

What did we learn from the quarter? (see pgs. 2 & 3 for full guide detail & add'l cost savings color)
DAN's intact 2025 guide includes sizable seq. revenue & margin step-ups, reflecting a combination of end-market stabilization & execution on DAN's robust $260M net cost savings (w/ +$175M achieved this year). DAN's outlook does not include any impact from the Off-Highway sale & we expect it to provide significant color on the 'NewCo' once a deal is formally announced (targeting early 2Q25, closing by year-end). DAN plans to formally re-segment for 1Q25 earnings, folding Power Tech into LV & CV, representing +$15M-$20M in overhead savings. While the guide does not include any U.S. tariff impact, DAN expects to fully pass along any incurred duties to its customers.

How did it change our investment thesis?
We remain highly encouraged by DAN's capital allocation potential following its Off-Highway sale and are further encouraged by commentary that DAN's tax rate will likely come down once the sale closes. We see ~$1.5B of post-sale shareholder returns potential (~62% of mkt. cap).

Estimates & price target changes ($21 PT reflects ~4.1x post-spin '26E EV/EBITDA)
Our '25E-'26E sales & EBITDA estimates remain largely unchanged, though our EPS runs higher to reflect the Co.'s lower than expected guided taxes w/ 2025E at a still-elevated ~42.5% adj. rate. With the OH sales' ~$2.7B in expected proceeds (~7x '26E EBITDA), our PT reflects ~4.1x EV/EBITDA on a post-spin '26E, vs. just ~3.1x at the current share price.
