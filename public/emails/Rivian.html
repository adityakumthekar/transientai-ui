RIVIAN AUTOMOTIVE (+)
Gross Profit Shines; 2025 Volume Outlook Embeds Prudent Conservatism
TP: USD18 • Upside: 32% • Price: USD13.6 (20 Feb. 25)

What happened?

RIVN reported 4Q24 adj. EBITDA losses of -$277M (adj. EPS of -$0.63) vs. our -$404M estimate (EPS -$0.74) and consensus of -$415M (EPS -$0.79), as positive gross profit far exceeded expectations (+$170M; 9.8% margin) compared to our +$53M estimate (3.5% margin), aided by in-line regulatory credit sales totaling $299M. ASPs finished +1.4% lower vs. our model (~$86.1K vs. BNPPE $84.9K). Importantly, RIVN in the qtr. saw its first revenue & positive gross profit accounting from its VW JV (est. ~$60M), which now will total $1.96B in associated revenue recognition (~30% gross margin) over the next 4 years. The Co. has begun separately reporting Software & Services from its Automotive operations, and will consist of the VW JV (accounting contribution), consumer & comm.'l subscriptions (e.g., Connect+, autonomy/ADAS, FleetOS), remarketing of trade-ins, and repair/maintenance. This revenue stream for the year is encouragingly slated to total $1B, at ~30% gross margins.

For 2025 guidance, RIVN conservatively expects to deliver ~46K-51K units (vs. BNPPE 57.7K; cons. 55.5K) and adj. EBITDA losses to total -$1.7B-$1.9B (vs. BNPPE -$1.85B; cons. -$1.65B), inclusive of confirmed "modestly positive" full-year gross profit (vs. BNPPE +$79M). 1Q deliveries are expected to represent the seasonal low at ~8K units, reflecting a combination of 4Q24's pull-forward (consumer & EDV), & the impact from LA fires. 2025 EDV shipments are expected to finish lower y/y after ‘24’s parts-related pull-forward with Amazon. RIVN plans to roll out its L2+ hands-off system in the coming weeks, expanding to the “vast majority” of urban & highway miles driven by the end of ‘25. RIVN has sourced 95% of the BoM for the R2 and anticipates an overall R2 BoM that halves the current Gen 2 R1.

BNPP Exane View:

We expect the shares to be supported Friday as solid gross profit & visibility into the Software & Services opportunity outweigh the conservative ’25 deliveries guide; Outperform ($18 PT). We see RIVN’s underwhelming deliveries outlook as conservative yet prudent given the significant regulatory uncertainty this year (w/ the Co. reasonably declining to specify the IRA & emissions change assumptions that underpin its outlook). We do see an opportunity for upside should a year-end IRA tax credit expiration incite material demand pull-forward. We are encouraged by the Co.'s guided $1B+ in Software & Services revenue at ~30% gross margins, which we believe is a key driver of RIVN’s ability to hit full-year gross profitability (as is the guided ~$300M in emissions credit sales). We note the Co. needs only one additional quarter of gross profit over $50M to unlock $1B of equity funding from Volkswagen at a 33% premium (see here for VW partnership details). RIVN also reaffirmed its target to achieve positive adj. EBITDA in 2027 (vs. BNPPPE -$171M)

– Guidance: RIVN’s 2025 guide calls for deliveries of 46K-51K (vs. BNPPE/consensus 57.7K/55.5K), adj. EBITDA losses of -$1.7B-$1.9B (vs. BNPPE/consensus -$1.85B/-$1.65B), and capex of ~$1.6B-$1.7B. The Co. continues to anticipate generating a modest gross profit in 2025.
