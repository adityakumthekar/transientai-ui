INDIA STRATEGY
2025 - the pricey paradise; cloudy skies

In our 2024 outlook 'The pricey paradise', we highlighted our cautious view on Indian equities. While Nifty 50 returns were in line with our expectations, we were wrong about mid and small caps. Backed by strong domestic flows, markets overcame negatives such as a slowdown in GDP and earnings growth, increase in capital gains tax, FII outflows and a large supply of equities. Broader markets still look pricey and the global and local macros look slightly less favourable. Our end-2025 Nifty 50 target of 25,500 implies 10% upside potential from current levels (8% for CY25). We prefer private sector Banks, IT, Telecoms and Consumer Discretionary to Staples, Pharma, Insurance and Metals.

Spike in US yields a negative for FII flows; recovery in GDP and earnings growth key
Global markets are reacting to the likely policies of the upcoming US administration. Indian equities have been off to a weak start in 2025 due to rising US bond yields which have led to the USD strengthening and FIIs selling in EMs. Though DII holdings are rising, India is not immune to FII selling as FII holdings are still 1.6x that of DIIs. Locally, earnings growth has slowed across sectors as the benefits of the post-pandemic reopening and commodity tailwinds are now behind. Slowing corporate tax and GST collections could constrain the government's ability to stimulate the economy while maintaining fiscal discipline. Our heat map comprising 50+ indicators indicates a slowdown in 2024, but also a bottoming out in 3QCY24.

Prefer large caps as the broader market is still expensive; we like Banks, IT and Telecoms
Our preference for large caps continues. We like banks as the FY26 earnings growth outlook is strong with no major credit-cost spikes on the anvil. The risk-reward balance looks favourable at below-median valuations. We also like IT Services as the macro environment is far more favourable than in the last two years. We like telecoms as the industry's FCF should continue to improve. In consumption, we continue to prefer affluent consumption to mass consumption (staples and 2Ws). We find Pharma expensive relative to earnings growth prospects. Our Metals outlook remains negative due to weak steel demand and prices.
