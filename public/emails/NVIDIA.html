NVIDIA (+)
Strong results/guide expected and kickstart a Rubin rally
TP: USD170 • Upside: 21% • Price: USD140.1 (20 Feb. 25)

A noisy two months since CES with lots of smoke but not much changed fundamentally. Expect strong results/guide and reassuring Blackwell supply/demand messaging. We like setup into GTC.

Expect classic beat/raise from NVDA vs sellside cons - FQ4 results due 26 Feb
Lots of smoke across the AI supply chain YTD, given overheating, bottlenecks and Hopper demand concerns. Commentary from the AI supply chain this reporting season was quite +ve and we think NVDA continues to ship and recognize revenues irrespective of any Blackwell teething supply issues. We expect classic beat/raise from NVDA vs FQ4 cons revenues of $38.3bn (+7% q/q, +70% y/y) and EPS of $0.84. Mgt indicated at CES of doing a bit better than expected in FQ4. For FQ1 we expect upside to the guide vs cons revenue of $42bn (+10% q/q, +62% y/y). This compares to buyside closer to $43bn. What could drive FQ1 upside potential is more Blackwell supply driving a richer mix and also renewed demand for Hopper, due to DeepSeek demand.

Investor concerns: Ramp-up cadence of different SKUs, Overheating, DeepSeek
Air pocket concerns persist among investors. Concerns of hyperscalers waiting for the next SKU, whether GB300 or Rubin, remain and hopefully get addressed on results. Given demand strength we don't think potential shifts is the SKU/mix matters at the group level. Overheating is a real issue in AI Datacenters and not going away anytime soon. See our recent MSFT (here) and supply chain (here) expert calls. Lastly, some investors remain concerned on the implications from DeepSeek. While very strong hyperscaler capex tells much of the story, we need to hear it from NVDA. In fact we think Hopper demand has increased as more users begin to use DeepSeek.

Rubin Rally? Strong results pave the way for stock to perform into GTC and Rubin launch
We think robust results and strong Blackwell supply/demand messaging will set the stock up nicely heading into GTC (Mar-17). Where we expect Rubin to be launched and CPO to see first adoption in NVDA Quantum switches.
