EU Earnings Momentum Is Building Up

BNPP Exane ALERT high frequency datapack. We collate and curate activity data from over 30 data sources on the US, Europe, and China, including key performance, flows, with earnings momentum trends and screens.

Here is a link to this week’s BNPP Exane Alert.

For stocks, earnings are everything. Hence, for European stocks to continue to outperform, beyond a short-lived positioning driven bounce, investors will need to see fundamental drivers to re-allocate meaningfully. So far, earnings momentum underpins the YTD performance. EPS upgrades and the breadth of these upgrades – at the sector or stock levels – suggest that momentum is building up. Multiple catalysts are likely to help both the fundamentals and valuations, but for now, earnings momentum remains cheap in the region (see page 7). Near-term catalysts: a potential ceasefire in Ukraine (Russia-Ukraine: The Peace Dividend) is the most talked about topic with investors with timing that remains uncertain, but the German elections are perhaps more tangible, this Sunday, with a potential for pro-growth measures.

Key takeaways:

– European Equities: More Than A Bounce? So far, the bulk of the outperformance has been driven by the relative PE normalisation. Not fully normalised and has further to go.

– Earnings are everything: For stocks, sustained outperformance needs earnings momentum.

– 8% EPS Growth Expected in 2025: After a mere 1.4% in 2024, consensus expects European EPS growth to catch up with US peers.

– EPS Uptrend Across Most Sectors: Broad improvement is visible with 12m fwd consensus EPS higher than 3m ago for most sectors.

– Revisions Breadth Is Supportive: A broader set of European stocks are seeing upgrades to their 12m fwd consensus EPS, suggesting momentum is likely to continue in the near term.

– Not Factored Into Valuations: Most European sectors are trading below their historical averages. Interestingly, some of the sectors with the best momentum – such as financials – are also some of the cheapest.

– Stock-Level Earnings Momentum: In our Stoxx 600 and S&P 500 dashboards, we highlight stocks leading on relative consensus earnings revisions (page 8) as well as the laggards (page 9).
