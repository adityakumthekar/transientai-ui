NUTRIEN (=)
Retail momentum improving into 2025
TP raised by 13% to USD54 • Upside: 3% • Price: USD52.5 (19 Feb. 25)

4Q sees Retail bouncing back
After cutting guidance for Retail in 3Q, the sizeable beat in 4Q was a welcome surprise. We understand from the company that the components of this were early realization of cost savings, better crop protection margins in North America and the beginnings of a recovery in Brazil.

2025 a better year for Retail - 2026 targets still look ambitious
Consensus for Retail EBITDA in 2024 was revised down by 16% through the course of last year. There are early signs in the 4Q print of cost programs coming through and ag fundamentals have turned more positive with the pickup in corn prices in particular. Mathematically, 4Q is typically 16%-23% of FY Retail EBITDA and extrapolating the 4Q24 result (exc. one-offs) would suggest Retail EBITDA of $1.72bn for 2025, exactly in line with consensus. Hence we feel Retail EBITDA is unlikely to see the same negative revisions through 2025. The 2026 target of $1.9-2.1bn still looks ambitious - but with consensus at $1.85bn for FY26, hitting this target is not in the price.

Elsewhere, geopolitics remains a question
Despite increasingly positive ag markets, Nutrien's Nitrogen and Potash divisions both face uncertainties from geopolitics, particularly relating to the Russia/ Ukraine war. Any peace deal could potentially see nitrogen cost curves flatten, reducing the cost advantage North American producers have had since 2022. On potash, whilst Russian tonnes are back in the market, there could potentially be incremental supply from Belarus which is currently sanctioned in Europe and the US and can't access its closest port (in Lithuania). Management expressed a view on the call that the port has been filled by other commodities, which could mean Belarus continues to occupy a higher position on the cost curve even in the event of a peace accord.

Price target
We increase our price target to $54 (from $48) as a result of our higher estimates.
