SEMICONDUCTORS AND IT HARDWARE
STMicroelectronics PIC100 Readthrough To The Optical Supply Chain

What happened?

ST Micro announced their first Silicon Photonics PIC (Photonic Integrated Circuit), PIC100, and called out AWS as a customer that is working with ST Micro and a leading optical module provider to use this product in data centers. PIC100 represents ST Micro’s attempt to be the leading foundry for module makers for SiPh-based optical transceivers. ST Micro claims PIC100 is the most efficient PIC on 300mm wafer and will ramp in 2H25, enabling 200G per lane optical links. The PIC100 also provides an intermediate step towards 400G per lane, enabling gradual infrastructure upgrades. Additionally, the company is working on through-silicon-via-bricks (TSV) and compact modulators to enable GPU-to-X optical interconnection so the PIC can be used in a chiplet configuration, hinting ST Micro is readying for CPO adoption.

BNPP Exane View:

Silicon photonic (SiPh) technology has historically been used for DCI and long-haul telecom applications, and Cisco dominated the market at 52% share followed by Intel at 33% in 2021. In our view, SiPh is now increasingly being used in 800G and 1.6T transceivers inside data centers, which is what we think ST Micro’s PIC100 targets. We think this announcement is greater validation of increasing demand for SiPh-based high performance transceivers inside data centers, which is positive for Coherent and more so for Lumentum given its greater exposure to EML/CW lasers.

Our checks with the optical supply chain indicate to us that Nvidia is leading the adoption of SiPh technology in its 1.6T DR8 optical transceivers (see here). We believe Fabrinet (+) is a key beneficiary of Nvidia adopting SiPh on the upcoming B300. ST Micro’s announcement indicates to us that AWS will ramp second gen 800G (200G/lane) single mode transceivers, and in the future 1.6T SiPh-based transceivers inside data centers using ST Micro’s PIC100, most likely Marvell’s DSPs, and third-party optical modules from the likes Lumentum, Coherent, or InnoLight. We think both Lumentum and Coherent are supplying transceivers to AWS. We note that Lumentum will ramp shipments of single mode 800G transceivers to a new customer in 2H25, and CloudLight has been focused in SiPh transceivers for Google.
