BESI (+)
The last cut for mainstream?
TP cut by 6% to EUR150 • Upside: 29% • Price: EUR116.2 (19 Feb. 25)

Weak mainstream business and 'HB Pause' lead to soft Q4 orders and Q1 guide
Besi Q4-24 rev/GM/EBIT (-4%/-110bps/-24% YOY) were largely in line with guide but 4-9% below css expectations. Focus was on orders (EUR121.9m/-28% YOY) which were the lowest level since Q2-23 and we think likely weaker than already cautious buy-side expectations (orders missed sell-side css by 29%), with Besi flagging lower hybrid bonding (HB) orders QOQ (BNPPE: <10 tools but orders doubling YOY in FY24) and an ongoing soft mainstream market (auto). On the latter, Besi expects no recovery until H2-25, reflected in a soft Q1-25 guide (0 to -10% rev QOQ, -17% vs. css at mid-point) but sees utilization rates among OSATs increasing as a good leading indicator for H2.

Samsung and Rapidus follow-on orders likely and HBM on verge of adoption?
Besi noted HB adoption continuing with now 15 customers (vs. 9 in 2023), with a first order from a Japanese customer (we think likely Rapidus) and a follow-up order from a Korean IDM (likely Samsung) for logic applications although these were flagged as low volume (R&D). Although orders can be lumpy, we expect further follow-on orders from these customers during the course of the year. We think the largest positive was comments about Samsung and Micron potentially adopting HB for HBM in the near-term (also with customers increasingly flagging heat dissipation issues). We think other HB demand drivers for 2025 are follow-on orders from TSMC (CPO, AMD, Apple, ASICs) and adoption among OSATs and the release of the new 50nm tool in H2.

We cut estimates and TP but remain Outperform on upcoming catalysts
We adjust estimates (EPS -18/-12% for FY25/26) and cut our TP to EUR150 (from EUR160). While Q1 opex is guided +10-20% QOQ (mainly relating to 'strategic consulting' cost), we expect opex growth to decrease substantially starting in Q2, mainly on lower SBC. With expectations on the mainstream market now reset, we think the June CMD, HB adoption in HBM, and follow-on orders from the now 15 customers will serve as catalysts, although uncertainty remains on the magnitude of those. Outperform maintained.
