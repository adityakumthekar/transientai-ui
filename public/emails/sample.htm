CONSUMER

 

FEEDBACK

Incomings this week focused upon UK retail as, despite in line to slightly better Xmas trading, investors are becoming increasingly nervous on the outlook for 2025. In addition to concern around demand (employment growth, higher costs of living with food inflation accelerating, rates), the stronger USD is a headwind for apparel companies sourcing in USD and opex inflation is likely to be meaningful in the context of wage cost growth. In terms of single stocks, we didn’t hear many defending any of the UK names this week – while investors understand M&S turnaround is in full swing (and they continue to take share), the lack of FY upgrade was an issue given 1) it is was a consensus long; 2) the next set of results is not until May. Feedback on Tesco was the only name leaning constructive – while some are concerned about rotation within grocery (out of Tesco, into Ahold Delhaize), most said they think accelerating UK food inflation will ultimately be another market share opportunity for Tesco (as they will continue to inflate below market). Investors were very disappointed by another quarter of LFL decline at B&M although we did hear of some short covering on the print on the idea that consumers might start trading down again to discounters. Elsewhere, feedback is generally “ok” on Luxury – I’m not getting much pushback on the “Q4 will be ok” thesis although people think visibility on Q1 is low and particularly heading into Chinese New Year. The assumption of most people is that Q1 will be worse due to tough comps but I’m starting to think messaging on current trading could be better than feared given 1) early timing of Chinese New Year, 2) some of the China high frequency data bottoming out. Last, feedback in Staples suggests no real step change in consensus positioning – for the sector overall and within it. Main pushback I’m getting on the likes of IMB, DGE, BN is that they are consensual longs.

INDUSTRIALS

FEEDBACK

Choppy week where it felt like conviction has been slow to form. Whilst the appetite for fresh ideas is high, last year’s themes seem hard to break away from, resulting in renewed enthusiasm for popular thematics like Eu Defence (GSSBDEFE +4% ytd), Civil Aero (GSXECIVA +2%), and Electrification (GSXEACDC +3%), whilst chems and autos renewed their underperformance after a brief (and almost record breaking) squeeze on Monday.  In the subsectors, chemicals interest remained concentrated in the higher quality or more “defensive” names, with incoming willing to defend recent underperformers among that group (DSFIR for example, where the stock is now down -20% from last year’s highs). In A&D, Trump comments pushing NATO to move defence spending to 5% of GDP prompted a big rally in the space, despite no evidence of this being a credible outcome or one that investors are willing to price in to the stocks. The upcoming German election (23rd Feb) is a the next important catalyst, in particular for RHM. In civil aero, lots of questions on whether valuation has simply run too far too fast (especially for after-market names), and some worries on Airbus after the strong run into year end that the stock could struggle to absorb a weak Q1 from these levels. In autos, quite a bit more push and pull, with specialists a lot less bearish in aggregate (bad news understood, valuations reset, stocks no longer going down on bad news), but still a lack of new interest from generalist money to revisit the space, especially as we await clarity on tariffs. Preference remains for stocks like Pirelli, Conti, or Renault that offer either an event angle or some insulation from China headwinds. In cap goods, electricals very much the focus of discussion, with GS house call on Prysmian really starting to get traction thanks to an FT article on Thursday highlighting potential US listing (already very much expected by the specialists, but seemed to act as a catalyst for new interest nonetheless). Siemens was also high on the list of topics of interest, with sales data hinting at signs of improvement in China automation and recent press reports of portfolio action being on the cards.  

 

FLOW

Activity mostly following the price action – buying of electrical and defence being a clear theme. We even saw some switching out of machinery into electricals, tempered with a bit of HF buying of upside in short-cycle machinery names ahead of results.
