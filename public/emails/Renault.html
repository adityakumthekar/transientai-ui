RENAULT (+)
2H24: Over to EU
TP cut by 4% to EUR63 • Upside: 23% • Price: EUR51.2 (19 Feb. 25)

Renault may no longer be the master of its own destiny as the margin support of new products fade, leaving the equity story more contingent in our view on external factors. However the latter could still prove potent for the stock, notably in the case of EU stimulus to the sector. We stay at Outperform.

What did we learn from H2'24?
Renault's largely in-line H2'24 report may raise more questions than it answered, with a FY25 guide for an EBIT margin >7% (incl. CO2 costs) easy to deem conservative were it not for a sharp negative swing in price/mix in the semester. To some degree this was expected, but investors have been accustomed to strong price/mix tailwinds at Renault in recent years as new products launched. This era may now be over as new launches focus on lower margin electrified products designed to help Renault's CO2 compliance in 2025. The company puts the compliance cost at c.100bp on margin, or c.EUR500m - this is largely in line with our estimated EUR400m headwind, which assumes some form of mild dilution as part of the upcoming EU Action Plan for the Auto Industry, due March 5th.

How does it change our investment view?
We continue to view Renault as well positioned to navigate the automotive complexities of 2025, with its minimal trade risk and China exposure making it a rare place of shelter from near-term headlines. Our positive view is however contingent on at least a modest CO2 reprieve being granted, else the risk of tough H2 pricing in Europe will likely see investors head for the exit in our view. Renault would however be a key beneficiary of any EU financial support to low-CO2 vehicles.

EBIT cut by 5/8% for '25/26e; TP trimmed to EUR63/share
We have cut our '25/26e EBIT by 5/8% respectively as we factor in slightly softer guidance, even if we still expect a margin of 7.5% in '25e (vs guide >7%). Our adj. EPS falls by a more material 12/22% for these years as we factor in the latest Nissan consensus estimates. Our target price falls by 5% to EUR63/share (from EUR66/share), and we retain our Outperform rating.
