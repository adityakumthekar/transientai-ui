AIR LIQUIDE (+)
Margin target raised again + big divi = high confidence
TP: EUR189 • Upside: 9% • Price: EUR172.8 (20 Feb. 25)

What happened?

- H2 Group EBIT came 1% above cons and our estimates, with slightly better margins. As seen with the other Industrial Gases, volumes were weak again in Q4, with pricing and efficiencies more than offsetting that. Note the margin surprise was stronger outside of Gases & Services (including a small one-off in GMT).

- Gases & Services organic growth was 1.9% vs cons at 2.7%. Merchant pricing is still high in Q4 (+3.6%), but we note weaker volumes in both Merchant and LI. Note this includes impact from small disposals (Africa for IM, cogen in Europe for LI).

- DPS set at €3.30, up 14% y/y and well above consensus of €3.10. Strong cash flow vs cons, net debt also slightly better.

- Backlog stayed at €4.2bn at Q4, the level reached in Q3. Note the projects announced last Monday (see Major decarb projects in Europe) should help support further growth going forward. Contribution from start-ups and ramp-ups in 2025 seen at €310-340m, vs our +€300m and the “>€250m” early look announce last Summer (at the time seen as underwhelming.)

- Guidance is vague as usual for the year ahead, with management committed to margin improvement. However, for the second year in a row they step-up the margin target extending the cumul improvement to 460bp over 5 years vs 320bp over 4 years previously. This implies +200bp over 2025/26, vs consensus of +140bp and our +150bp. In the slide the indicate 100bp as a new threshold which to us implies they target 100bp for both 2025 and 2026.

BNPP Exane View:

Beyond the robust H2, we note clear signs of confidence from both the large dividend increase and the new 2026 margin target. We suspect small upgrades can come from a combination of margins and FX, more than compensating for a volumes outlook that remains uncertain. We really like the fact that management is not waiting for a full CMD to provide a direction of travel on the profitability, this is a clear sign of market friendliness in our view. We expect consensus to show marginal upgrades, with more coming in 2026 than 2025. We think positioning has been balanced and would expect shares to react positively although not to the same extent as last year when the margin target was doubled.

We suspect focus on the call will be drawn to (1) drivers of the margin improvement, (2) outlook for volumes and new projects in decarb and electronics, and (3) capital allocation, given ongoing deleveraging. Note we will host management for meetings next week with CEO and CFO. There will be a sell-side meeting on Monday as well.
