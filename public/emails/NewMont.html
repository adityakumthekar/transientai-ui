NEWMONT (+)
4Q24 first take - strong quarterly beats, costs still rising into 2025
TP: USD57 • Upside: 19% • Price: USD48.1 (20 Feb. 25)

What happened?

Newmont reported a production led beat in 4Q24 which fed down into subsequent beats at adjusted EBITDA and FCF. 2025 guidance for gold production at 5.6Moz (core portfolio) is in-line with prior communications though cash costs and AISC are now pointing to y/y increases and are c5% above consensus.

BNPP Exane View:

Newmont reported 4Q24 attributable gold production of 1.9Moz, +6% vs BNPPE and +9% vs Bloomberg consensus. The beat was driven primarily from Penasquito, Boddington and Tanami. Gold costs applicable to sales (USD 1,096/oz) and AISC (USD 1,466/oz) were largely in-line with expectations.

Adjusted EBITDA of USD 3.1bn was a +13% and +16% beat vs BNPPE and consensus, owing primarily to the better production figure. Adjusted EPS of USD 1.40/sh was +15% and +32% vs BNPPE and consensus. The quarterly divi of USD 0.25/sh was in-line. Capex of USD USD 875m was in-line while FCF of USD 1.6bn was significantly ahead of expectations (+160% vs BNPPE and +71% vs consensus). It was primarily the earnings beat dropping down which drove the beat on FCF despite WC being a relative headwinds vs expectations. Leverage remains low at 0.6x LTM adjusted EBITDA.

2025 production guidance of 5.6Moz (5.9Moz incl non-core assets) was in-line with their previously communicated outlook. Guidance on costs is now pointing to an increase y/y vs prior communication for a more flat performance given the expected flat production profile. Cash cost guidance for the tier 1 portfolio is guided to USD 1,180/oz (+5% above consensus) and AISC at USD 1,620/oz (also +5% vs consensus). Capex guidance of USD 3.2bn was in-line (consensus USD 3.2bn). On balance, cash flow in 2025 is guided to be H2 weighted as the first half of the year is expected to achieve 48% of attributable gold production but will see elevated spend in sustaining capital (52%) and development capital (57%).

YE24 attributable gold reserves 125Moz adjusted for assets held for sales compares to 136Moz at YE23 (unadjusted for asset sales). Increases of +7Moz due to price and cost (gold assumption moved to USD 1,700/oz vs USD 1,400/oz prior) and +3Moz from net additions at certain assets offset by -4Moz net revisions at certain assets, -8Moz depletion and -9Moz for the assets held for sale. The price assumption of USD 1,700/oz is 17% below the trailing 3-year average price, vs the prior ten-year period having a reserve price assumption c.13% below the trailing 3-year average. The average gold grade is effectively flat y/y at 0.96 g/t.
